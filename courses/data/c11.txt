DESC|A course on the deep structure of political conflict: the two moral grammars (rights and entitlements versus inheritance and duties), the psychology beneath them, and the rhetorical weaponry built on top — empathy converted into a targeting system, inversion tactics, the myth of neutrality, and the compassion-vocabulary of history's worst regimes. The charter, stated plainly: this course studies the left at its worst, not the left as such; every tactic lesson runs the symmetric audit and names the right's version of the same move, because the goal is to see structures, not to hate tribes. Case files steelman the genuine underlying debate before dissecting the move. Closes with craft: what to actually do, with Jordan Peterson's stands studied and audited like every other thinker on this platform. House style: the Honest Counterpoint ends every lesson.
