DESC|A braided course that tells two stories at once. One thread is the systematic theology of the Reformed tradition — Scripture, the God who reigns, grace alone, the eternal decree, Christ and his saving work, the application of redemption, the church, and the last things. The other is the history of the Reformation that recovered it — from the late-medieval eve through Luther, Zwingli, and Calvin's Geneva, out across France, the Netherlands, Scotland, and England, into the confessions, the wars of religion, and the tradition's long reach. Roughly half pure Reformed theology and half Reformation history, the two threads alternate so that each illuminates the other: the doctrine and the drama of its rediscovery side by side. Confessionally Reformed in conviction, but intellectually honest — engaging the strongest objections and representing other traditions fairly where they differ.
