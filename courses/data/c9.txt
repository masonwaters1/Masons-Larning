SUBTITLE|The Man, the Theory, the Body Count, and the Idea That Won't Die
ACCENT|#6e1423

DESC|A rigorous, unflinching examination of Marxism from a free-market and classically liberal perspective: the life and system of Karl Marx; why the theory fails on its own terms and against human nature; the full historical record of the regimes built in its name, told with names, dates, and numbers; the great critics — Sowell, Hayek, Mises, Friedman, Kolakowski, Orwell, Solzhenitsyn — given full-length treatment; the psychology of the ideology's appeal, examined through a Jordan Peterson lens among others; and the question of why an idea refuted by argument and history alike keeps returning. The course's discipline is the same as its sister seminars: every claim anchored to a citable source, the strongest counterarguments built in and answered rather than avoided, and the honest distinction maintained between Marxism and the democratic welfare state — because a critique that flattens Sweden into Stalin discredits itself. The verdict is not assumed; it is earned, lesson by lesson.
