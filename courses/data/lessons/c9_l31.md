The unit closes with the defense that concedes everything this unit has documented and saves the doctrine anyway — the most successful argument in the history of apologetics, heard in every seminar and comment thread where the subject arises: real communism has never been tried. Its logic: what Marx meant by communism — a stateless, classless, democratic association of free producers — describes none of the regimes in this catalogue; what existed was Stalinism, state capitalism, a bureaucratic deformation Marx would have despised; therefore the record convicts the impostors and leaves the idea untouched, still waiting, still innocent. The course takes the argument at full strength — it will be steelmanned below, including the honorable roll of leftists who opposed the regimes from the beginning — because it deserves a real answer rather than a scoff, and because the answer, once assembled from this course's own prior findings, is the unit's true summation: the defense fails not on one ground but on five convergent ones, and what survives of it — a genuine remainder the course will state precisely — turns out to sharpen the indictment rather than soften it.

## The Defense at Full Strength

State it properly.[^1] Marx's mature vision (L8) was explicitly anti-statist: the state withers; the free development of each is the condition for the free development of all; nothing in the Critique of the Gotha Programme describes a secret police. The regimes of this unit were one-party dictatorships over the proletariat, not of it; several serious left traditions said so from the start — Rosa Luxemburg, in prison in 1918, warning Lenin that "freedom only for the supporters of the government... is no freedom at all," and that suppressing pluralism must end in "a clique affair — a dictatorship... of a handful of politicians"; the anarchists and Kronstadt sailors (L20) demanding soviets without commissars; the Mensheviks, the left communists, later Trotsky's heirs and the democratic socialists of the West, who fought Stalinism decades before its Western admirers awoke. Add the conditions argument: Marx predicted communism for advanced industrial societies, and every trial ran in poor, war-wrecked, autocratic ones under siege — the theory, the defense concludes, was never tested on its own terms. That is the case at its best, and pieces of it are true. Now the audit.

## The Five Answers

First, the induction. The doctrine has been implemented by revolutionaries of every temperament, in some four dozen states and movements, across every inhabited continent, every culture, every religion's former territory, every development level from agrarian Cambodia to industrial Czechoslovakia, over a span of seventy years — and the outcome converged, in every single case, on the same architecture: vanguard party-state, political police, category enemies, camps, and either terror sustained or reform away from the doctrine. A hypothesis that fails in one country has an excuse; in five, a pattern; in forty out of forty, a law. At some sample size — and forty across all conditions is past it — "never tried" inverts into the only scientifically respectable reading: always becomes. The dose-response finding (L26) completes the induction with its gradient: the more fully and rapidly the program was applied, the higher the death rate — the correlation running in exactly the wrong direction for the betrayal theory, which requires the purest attempts to be the gentlest. Second, the mechanism — because the convergence is not a statistical mystery but a derivation this course has already performed: abolish market exchange and prices, and calculation fails (L12), producing shortage; shortage plus the ineradicable market instinct (L13) produces black markets; suppressing them requires economic police; requisitioning without prices requires coercion (L20); administering the propertyless economy requires administrators, who become — with no property to check them and no elections to remove them — precisely the new class Bakunin predicted in 1873 (L8) and the Yugoslav vice-president Milovan Djilas, from inside the highest circle of a ruling party, documented in 1957 in the book that cost him his freedom and named the phenomenon for good: The New Class, the nomenklatura as history's least accountable ruling stratum, owning everything collectively and answering to no one. The police state, in short, is not communism's betrayer but its load-bearing wall: the "distortion" is what implementation consists of, which is why it appeared everywhere, under leaders who hated each other, and why the one instruction manual that could have prevented it — how to abolish markets without coercion — has never been written by anyone, including Marx, whose silence on transition mechanics (L8) is where every dictatorship poured in. Third, the epistemic structure: the defense is Popper's conventionalist twist (L16) in historical costume — a theory whose every implementation is disqualified retroactively, by its failure, from counting as an implementation cannot be tested even in principle; and the retroactivity is documented: each regime in this catalogue was hailed as the real thing by the world movement while it appeared to succeed (the Webbs' Soviet Communism: A New Civilisation?, famously dropping its question mark in the second edition at the Terror's height; Sartre on the Soviet citizen's freedom; the Cuba and China pilgrimages; Cambodia defended until the Vietnamese opened the camps) and excommunicated after failure, on a schedule set by the failure's publicity — 1956, 1968, 1989 — never in advance. The course therefore puts to the defense the question Popper taught (L16): state now, before the next attempt, what would count as communism being tried — and observe that no answer has ever been given that the last forty cases would not already satisfy. Fourth, the founders' fingerprints: the regimes did not freelance their machinery — the dictatorship of the proletariat, the revolutionary terror endorsements, the suspension of law, the press suppressions, the class-category enemy are in the founders' texts and practice (L8, L9, L19's chain), and Lenin's system implemented the corpus he knew by heart; "Marx would have hated Stalin" may be true (the Amsterdam speech's democratic hints are banked in L8), but paternity is not approval — the question is not whether the architect would have liked the building but whether the blueprint, followed, produces it, and forty sites say it does. Fifth, symmetry: this course judges capitalism by its record — the slave ledgers, Bengal, the Congo fill its sister volumes — not by Bastiat's ideals; the defense demands communism be judged by its ideal against capitalism's record, a double standard that dissolves the moment it is named: judge both by ideals, and no system has ever failed; judge both by records, and the unit's accounting (L30) stands.

## The Honest Remainder

What survives, stated with the course's discipline, because something does. It is true that Marx's terminal vision — stateless, classless abundance — has never existed; the course's claim has never been that it was achieved and found wanting, but that the attempt to reach it produces, lawfully, the transition-state that kills — the destination is not disproven; it is unreachable by the only road the doctrine builds, and the road is the crime scene. It is true that democratic socialism within liberal constitutional orders — parties that accept elections, courts, and mixed economies — is a different animal, and this course will judge it separately and on its own record (Unit 6's assignment; the reader should note now that the Nordic states routinely conscripted into this argument are, by their own governments' insistence, market economies with large welfare states — "Denmark is far from a socialist planned economy," in the Danish prime minister's own weary correction — which relocates rather than settles that debate). And it is true, and honorable, that the left's own dissidents saw first: Luxemburg's prison warnings, the Kronstadt resolution, Orwell returning from Catalonia, the Mensheviks and anarchists who filled the early camps — the course banks their credit in full, and then enters the observation that completes the unit: their warnings were internal, early, precise — and structurally unheeded, because the system they warned had already abolished the mechanisms (elections, press, faction) by which warnings change anything; the existence of the prophets is the final evidence against the defense, for it shows the outcome was foreseen from within the tradition itself, in real time, and the doctrine proceeded anyway — not for want of warning, but by design that made warning inert.

## The Honest Counterpoint

The strongest surviving form of the defense deserves the closing word and answer: conditions were always wrong — poor countries, sieges, inherited autocracies; advanced-country communism remains untested, and the sieges themselves were partly the capitalist world's doing. The answer has three parts. The theory itself staked its scientific standing on advanced countries going first (L4's graded predictions); their refusal is the theory's falsification, not its excuse — a doctrine that only ever wins power where its own theory says it cannot work has already told the analyst something. The siege is substantially endogenous: a movement whose founding documents declare the forcible overthrow of all existing social conditions has announced the war it then cites in mitigation — hostility to a self-proclaimed mortal enemy is not an experimental confound but the predictable response the doctrine's own texts guarantee (and the sieges, the record shows, never prevented the terror's domestic targets from being teachers, peasants, and party members rather than invaders). And the advanced tests exist: Czechoslovakia entered 1948 as an industrial parliamentary democracy with a large working class — the theory's ideal candidate — and produced show trials, police state, and 1968's tanks on schedule; East Germany ran beside West Germany as the cleanest advanced-world control pair after Korea (L27), same people, same rubble, and the wall was built to hold the experiment's subjects in. The unit's verdict is entered: the record is the doctrine's, by induction, mechanism, epistemic structure, paternity, and symmetry — and the remainder belongs to those who fought it, many from the left, all documented. The course now turns from the crime scene to the witnesses for the prosecution: the thinkers who saw it coming, or saw through it from inside, beginning with the economist who called the century in 1920 and was mocked for seventy years until the wall came down on schedule — Mises, next, and Unit 4.
