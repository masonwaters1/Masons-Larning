The anatomy left one question open, and it is the deepest in the course: why do the intellectuals, of all people, keep the faith? The doctrine's economics fell a century ago (L11), its predictions failed publicly (L4), its regimes filled Unit 3's graves, and its most refuted status survives — not among the workers it claimed, who abandoned it wherever they could vote or flee, but in the faculties, the quality press, and the seminar: the constituency with the most training in evidence retaining the creed most refuted by it. Sowell's answer occupies the books that complete his arc — The Vision of the Anointed (1995) and Intellectuals and Society (2009) — and it is not a sneer but a theory: an incentive-and-accountability analysis of the idea trade itself, applying to intellectuals exactly the materialist scrutiny Marx applied to everyone else. Its core: intellectuals are dealers in ideas whose end products are ideas — a occupational category defined by the absence of the feedback that disciplines every other trade, since their products are tested by peer applause rather than by consequences that return to the producer; and Marxism is not one temptation among many for such a class but the temptation engineered to its specifications — the doctrine that flatters intellectuals with history's starring role, re-ranks the world's knowledge to their advantage, and immunizes their verdicts against the tests they would otherwise fail. This lesson states the theory, runs it against the fellow-traveler case file the course has assembled, and then — per the standing rule — turns the instrument on the course's own side.

## The Accountability Gap

The definitional move first, because everything follows from it.[^1] Sowell defines intellectuals occupationally, not honorifically: those whose work begins and ends in ideas — the philosopher, the critic, the columnist, the theorist — as distinct from those who deal in ideas that must cash out in the world (the engineer whose bridge stands or falls, the physician whose patient recovers or doesn't, the entrepreneur whose forecast meets the market). The distinction identifies not intelligence but a feedback structure: the engineer's errors return to the engineer; the intellectual's errors are absorbed by distant strangers, while the intellectual's standing is set by the only audience that scores him — other intellectuals; and a profession scored by peers rather than consequences will select, over time, for what impresses peers: novelty, moral elevation, and what Sowell calls verbal virtuosity — the ability to make a position glitter being rewarded whether or not the position survives contact with the world. From the structure follow the pathologies the earlier lessons previewed: the teflon prophet (Sowell's case studies of celebrated experts serially, catastrophically wrong — the population-bomb school the canonical exhibit — whose standing rose with each falsified forecast, because the applause meter and the accuracy meter are different instruments); the crusade cycle of The Vision of the Anointed (the "crisis" declared over the data's objection, the "solution" enacted, the results ignored or redefined, the critics' motives indicted — the four stages this course watched at L4 and at Lushan); and the "mascots and targets" pattern — the poor deployed as exhibits for the vision rather than principals whose actual outcomes are measured (the Puerto Rican cane cutters of L35 are the arc's primal scene: the program's beneficiaries in rhetoric, its casualties in data, and the data unwanted). None of this, note, is a theory of intellectuals' villainy; it is L13's incentive analysis applied to the idea trade — systems select behavior, and a system that never invoices its members for error will carry error at scale.

## Why Marxism Fits the Trade

Now the specific attraction, and Sowell's account (with Hayek's 1949 essay "The Intellectuals and Socialism" as its companion) gives four interlocking mechanisms. First, the flattery of role: Marxism assigns the intellectual history's leading part — consciousness must be brought to the workers "from without" (Lenin's formula, L8); the vanguard, the theorist, the one who has seen through appearances is the revolution's indispensable officer — and L17's surrogate-decision-maker vision generalizes it: a creed in which the enlightened few must decide for the conditioned many is structurally a job description, addressed to precisely the class that gets to answer it. Second, the re-ranking of knowledge: Hayek's theorem (L33) established that the market order runs on dispersed, tacit, practical knowledge — the foreman's, the shipper's, the housewife's — which is exactly the knowledge intellectuals do not hold; planning, by contrast, is the regime of articulated knowledge — reports, theories, plans, the intellectual's native medium — so that the choice between market and plan is also, and not incidentally, a choice between knowledge-classes, and the intelligentsia's chronic preference for the plan is, on Sowell's materialist reading, its class interest speaking in the vocabulary of compassion. Third, the immunity: the unfalsifiable structure Unit 2 dissected (L16) is, for the working intellectual, a professional armor — a total system that pre-processes every objection into evidence (false consciousness for the workers who disagree, class position for the colleagues who do) can never lose a seminar, and the trade's selection pressure (win the room) favors armored systems over testable ones. Fourth, the moral economy: the vision confers, at zero behavioral cost, the trade's supreme good — differential righteousness: to hold the view is to be the kind of person who holds it, and the doctrine's actual record (Unit 3) is billed, per L31's mechanism, to impostors, so the account never comes due. Run the theory against the case file this course has assembled and it fits like a key: the Webbs mistaking the Terror's paperwork for civilization, Duranty's Pulitzer, Sartre's pilgrimages, the Cambodia skeptics (L26), the mourning asymmetry (L29–L30) — each a specimen of consequences falling on distant strangers while the standing was set by the room; and Orwell's sentence, written from inside the intelligentsia and two lessons early, stands as the theory's epigraph: some ideas are so foolish that only an intellectual could believe them, "for no ordinary man could be such a fool."

## The Vocabulary of the Anointed

The theory's most portable equipment is Sowell's audit of the trade's language, because verbal virtuosity (the section above) works through a standing lexicon the reader can learn to hear. The inventory's principle: the anointed's words do the arguing before any argument begins — verdicts smuggled in as vocabulary. "Compassion" denotes the disposition to spend — typically other people's money — with the disposition itself scored as the virtue, results unaudited (stage four of the crusade cycle never arrives for the compassionate, since the word certified them at stage one). "Greed" attaches to keeping what one has earned but never to demanding what others have earned — an asymmetry Sowell delights in flipping: the man who wants to keep his wages is greedy; the man who wants those wages redirected to himself or his causes is idealistic. "Public service" ennobles the sector with the least feedback (L13's incentive analysis of the reformers) while the private trades that feed and clothe the public rank as self-interest — a valuation exactly inverted from the accountability structures involved. "Society" functions as the address to which intractable problems are mailed ("society's failure"), converting tragedy into indictment and licensing the surrogate decision-makers (L17) to answer the mail. Dismissal words police the border: tested, constrained-vision arguments are "simplistic" (trade-offs always sound simplistic beside solutions), while armored systems are "nuanced"; critics "blame the victim" when they measure outcomes, and every disparity becomes a "crisis" requiring the anointed's management. The reader will notice that the lexicon is the immunizing machinery of L16 miniaturized to the word level — each term a small conventionalist twist, pre-processing evidence into confirmation — and will notice, next lesson, that the greatest essay ever written on this mechanism came from the left's own truth-teller: Orwell's "Politics and the English Language" is the same audit conducted on the same trade, which is why the course schedules them back to back.

## The Instrument Turned Around

The course's standing rule (L16, L30) now binds: an analysis this powerful must be aimed symmetrically or it becomes what it indicts. Three applications. The right has its anointed: the accountability gap does not check party cards — the think-tank fellow serially wrong about deficits or wars at no professional cost, the market-anointed crusade that ignores its own stage-four data, the invocation of "freedom" as a verbal-virtuosity trump are the same pathology in different colors, and the course's tradition harbors teflon prophets whose forecasts the reader should audit by name and date exactly as Sowell audits Ehrlich's. The genetic-fallacy guard: explaining a belief's attraction is not refuting it — Sowell's class analysis of the intelligentsia would be, alone, precisely the ad hominem this course convicted at L9 and L16; its legitimacy here rests on the order of operations the arc has enforced — the demolition on the merits came first (L11–L18, L36), and the sociology explains only the survival of the already-refuted; the reader must keep that sequence or surrender the method. And the self-referential test: Sowell is an intellectual, this course is an intellectual product, and the escape from the paradox is the criterion the theory actually proposes — not occupation but accountability: the distinction runs between ideas staked against feedback (Mises's 1920 prediction, Friedman's 1967, the falsifiable cores this unit has flagged) and ideas armored against it; the course's own standard, announced for Unit 6 and binding retroactively, is that its claims be of the first kind, and the reader is deputized to enforce it.

## The Honest Counterpoint

Three entries beyond the symmetry already applied. First, the anti-intellectualism risk: the theory is one demagogue's-cudgel away from "ignore the educated," a reading Sowell's own life refutes (the arc's witness is a scholar answering scholars with more scholarship) and the course forecloses explicitly: the target is a feedback structure, not learning; the remedy is accountability, not philistinism — and the tradition's own heroes (Unit 4's roster) are intellectuals whose distinction was submitting to tests. Second, the theory's empirical status: "intellectuals lean left because planning flatters them" is itself hard to test cleanly (survey data confirm the lean; the mechanism competes with alternatives — self-selection, campus ecology, the historical accident of who owned the salons), and the course grades it as a well-evidenced interpretive framework rather than a demonstrated law — powerful enough to organize the case file, honest enough to say so. Third, the noble exceptions are load-bearing: the fellow-traveler file has a counter-file — Orwell, Aron, Kolakowski, Camus, the Congress for Cultural Freedom's dissidents — intellectuals who paid full professional price to tell the truth about the left from the left, and their existence both tempers the class indictment and sharpens the question the unit's remaining lessons answer in portraits: what made the exceptions except themselves? The arc closes with its finding entered — the climate of opinion (L36) persists because the trade that maintains climates is structured to reward it — and the unit turns to the exceptions themselves: first the philosopher who taught the doctrine at its highest level inside the bloc, and then spent a thousand pages burying it as "the greatest fantasy of our century." Kolakowski, next.
